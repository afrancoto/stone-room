A listening lab

Stone Room

Twenty-three rooms. In the lab rooms, the difference shrinks trial by trial until it fades — the fade point is your measurement, in Hz and dB, saved per headphone so pairs can be compared.

Before you start: switch OFF Dolby Atmos (Settings → Sounds and vibration → Sound quality and effects) and any EQ. Phone spatializers fight this game's own 3D rendering and smear the positions.

First, a quick check

Left. Centre. Right.

Three notes travel across the stage. If they clearly move ear to ear, you're set. If they don't — or left and right feel smeared — turn off Dolby Atmos and check Mono audio is off.

One more thing

Which headphones are on?

Results are saved per pair, so you can run the same rooms on different headphones and compare them.

Comparing pairs? Match loudness first: replay the calibration notes on each pair and set the phone volume until they sound equally loud. Keep ANC in the same mode, and check both use their best Bluetooth codec (Developer options → Bluetooth audio codec).
