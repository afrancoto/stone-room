# Stone Room — competitive research digest (2026-07-16)

Method: multi-agent deep research; every claim below survived 3-vote adversarial
verification against primary sources unless marked otherwise. Full trail in the session
transcript.

## The landscape splits into two camps that never overlap

**Listener-trainers** (train your ears, ignore your gear):
- **Quiztones** — the leading consumer EQ trainer. iOS/Mac only (Android app unpublished
  since Dec 2021), freemium (~$4.99/mo). 450+ quizzes, strictly EQ/gain — no soundstage,
  transients, timing, or spatial content despite user requests.
- **Harman "How to Listen"** — the only free research-grade trainer, but it's 2011 desktop
  abandonware (Windows XP-era, Mac 10.5+), never web or mobile. Adaptive difficulty, but
  its output is listener skill level — never a device measurement.
- **PSYCHOACOUSTICS-WEB** (Behavior Research Methods, 2024) — academic proof that Stone
  Room's exact paradigm (browser, mobile, Levitt adaptive forced-choice staircases) is
  valid science. Pure-tone lab stimuli, zero gamification, measures the listener only.

**Device-measurers** (measure reference units, ignore your ears):
- **AudioCheck.net** — closest incumbent. Free browser test-signal suite incl. a dedicated
  headphone battery (bass/treble extension, driver matching, distortion, binaural) and
  stereo/localization demos. But everything is static files: no scoring, no adaptivity,
  no saved results. Its bass test — a self-reported sweep — is the market's closest
  analogue to a Stone Room fade point, and it saves nothing.
- **squig.link / Crinacle** — lab-rig measurement databases (~120 federated sites). They
  characterize a sample unit on a standardized coupler — never your unit, on your head,
  through your ears. Zero training content. Complementary, not competitive.

## (a) Where competitors shine — and the catch-up path

| Their strength | Why catch-up is fast |
|---|---|
| AudioCheck: signal breadth, incumbency | Static files; Stone Room already matches most coverage with strictly better method (scored, adaptive, saved) |
| Quiztones: 450+ quizzes, real-music import | Volume, not depth — add rooms/variants over time; real-music stimuli is the one genuinely valuable idea worth borrowing |
| Harman: research-validated pedagogy | Its training design is published and freely study-able |
| squig.link: community scale | Don't compete — complement. Position Stone Room results *alongside* rig graphs; seek links from squig-adjacent communities |

The one asset not replicable solo is squig.link's community. Everything else is static,
platform-locked, or dormant.

## (b) Differentiators Stone Room already has (in order of market rarity)

1. Adaptive 2AFC staircases producing quantified per-headphone thresholds in Hz/dB —
   **absent in every verified consumer competitor**.
2. Saved results enabling pair-vs-pair comparison of *your own* headphones through
   *your own* ears.
3. Device measurement + listener training unified in one product (the market is strictly
   bifurcated).
4. Free, mobile-first, gamified web delivery.

## (c) The most under-communicated strength

Every other message space is occupied — "train your ears" (Quiztones/Harman), "free
browser headphone test" (AudioCheck), "compare headphones" (squig/Crinacle). The one claim
with **zero occupants**: *"your headphones, measured by your ears, in Hz and dB — saved,
and comparable pair to pair."* Lead with the measurement-output framing everywhere
(page copy, share text, any future posts); ear-training framing buries the only claim no
incumbent can echo.

## Caveats (honest edges of the research)

- SoundGym, TrainYourEars, iZotope Pro Audio Essentials, Philips Golden Ears, ABX tools,
  and Mimi produced **no claims that survived verification** — "absent across the market"
  means absent across the *verified* set. SoundGym is the biggest hole: it's the most
  gamified competitor and its retention mechanics (leagues, streaks) remain unstudied.
- Refuted, do not reuse: "Harman How to Listen covers spatial training across 17 tasks"
  (that was Harman's internal program, not the released software); "AudioCheck targets
  only engineers, leaving beginner education uncontested" (it has general-audience
  training content too).
- Vendor figures (450+ quizzes, 120 sites) are marketing counts as of 2026-07-16.
