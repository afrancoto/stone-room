Everything you hear passes through five stages
The recordingStreams and files are lossy-compressed — the encoder discards what a masking model predicts you won’t hear. A choice made before you press play.
The phone & its appsVolume curve, EQ, spatializers, system mixing — every setting quietly reshapes the signal.
The connectionBluetooth re-compresses the audio again (SBC, AAC, aptX, LDAC — codecs differ); a cable skips that step.
The headphonesThe driver’s frequency response, distortion, and seal against your head.
Your earsOuter-ear filtering, your own hearing thresholds, your training. Where sound becomes perception.

A lab rig measures the headphone stage in isolation — precisely, but alone. Listening happens through all five at once. Stone Room measures the whole line, because the whole line is what you hear — which is why every reading here says you + this chain, never a bare headphone spec.

Which headphones are on?

Results are saved per pair, so you can run the same rooms on different headphones and compare them.

Comparing pairs? Match loudness first: replay the calibration notes on each pair and set the phone volume until they sound equally loud. Keep ANC in the same mode, and check both use their best Bluetooth codec (Developer options → Bluetooth audio codec).
